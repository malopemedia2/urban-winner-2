TX Theatre: A Decade of Vision, Passion, and Perseverance

What started as a simple thought more than ten years ago has grown into a thriving force in South African theatre. TX Theatre, the brainchild of Mxolisi Masisela and Mbongeni Mabunda, was born out of a shared passion for creating meaningful, engaging, and transformative theatre experiences.

In its earliest days, TX Theatre was little more than an idea, a dream waiting to find form. The path was not easy. For years, Masisela and Mabunda labored tirelessly, often under challenging circumstances, navigating the difficulties of limited resources, audience development, and building a reputation in a competitive cultural landscape.

Their journey was marked by sacrifice, patience, and an unwavering belief in the power of theatre to inspire and transform communities. Through perseverance, TX Theatre slowly carved out a space for itself in South Africa’s cultural scene. Their commitment to quality productions, mentorship of young artists, and engagement with local communities has paid off in ways that far exceed their early ambitions.

One of their most significant achievements is Thembisa Theatre Week, which has grown from a modest local initiative into the most successful theatre week in the region. The event now attracts performers, directors, and audiences from across the country, celebrating creativity and fostering new talent.

Looking back over ten years, TX Theatre’s journey reflects a story of resilience, vision, and the transformative power of art. What was once a mere idea is now a thriving institution, impacting countless lives, nurturing emerging artists, and setting a standard for excellence in South African theatre.

The founders’ dream has become a reality—a testament to what can be achieved with passion, determination, and the courage to keep going even when the road is long and uncertain.

As TX Theatre moves into its next decade, it carries forward a legacy of inspiration, community engagement, and artistic excellence. The story of Masisela, Mabunda, and TX Theatre reminds us that great achievements often start as small thoughts—and with persistence, those thoughts can change the cultural landscape forever.
